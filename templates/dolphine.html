The pink dolphin

Pink Dolphins (Boto)
Habitat and Behavior:
- Habitat: Amazon and Orinoco rivers.
- Behavior: Small groups (2-4 individuals) and more solitary than marine dolphins.
- Communication: Clicks and whistles, echolocation.
Adaptation and Abilities:
- Environmental Adaptation: Adapt to seasonal floods.
- Movement: Swim in shallow and flooded areas due to flexible bodies.
Diet:
- Feeding: Fish, crustaceans, some invertebrates. Skilled hunters using echolocation.
Threats and Conservation:
- Threats: Pollution, habitat loss, illegal hunting.
- Conservation Efforts: Awareness programs, river monitoring, strengthening laws.
Interesting Facts:
- Coloraton:pink color becomes more pronounced in older males, intensifies during physical activity.
